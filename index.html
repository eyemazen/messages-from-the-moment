About the Book

What Is
This Book?

You already know something is off. You're busy, you're trying, you're doing everything right and still something feels missing. Like you're on a treadmill you can't step off of.

Messages From the Moment was made for exactly that feeling.

Nine years of original artwork. Fifteen years on the meditation cushion. 57 illustrated stories, each written without revision, pulled straight from the moment it came from.

At art fairs across New York and Connecticut, strangers would choose a piece intuitively, then read the story behind it. Every time, the reaction was the same. The words weren't just relatable. They spoke directly to what that person was carrying at that exact moment in their life. Something they hadn't yet been able to name.

This book does the same thing. It won't tell you what to fix. It will stop the mental loop long enough for you to breathe, feel like yourself again, and remember something you already know but keep forgetting. That you are enough. That you are on the right path. And that this moment, exactly as it is, is where your life actually lives.

— James (Client)

About the Author

Christian
Rosstad

Christian Rosstad is a digital artist, writer, and mindfulness teacher. He has spent fifteen years learning how to get out of his own way. That practice became his art. His art became this book.

For nearly a decade he has created using a one-shot approach. No revision. No overthinking. Each piece drawn directly from the moment, guided by intuition rather than perfection. The stories in this book were written exactly the same way. Not to be analyzed. To be felt.

He holds a Level 1 MBSR Teacher Training through Brown University. But what you feel in these pages didn't come from a classroom. It came from fifteen years of showing up, sitting down, and doing the real work.

His work has been shown at Pratt Institute, shared with thousands of people online, and has a way of finding people at the exact moment they need it most. Once you read it, you will understand why.

This book is not meant to be read in a linear way or mastered. It is meant to meet you where you are. The message that finds you is the one you need.
